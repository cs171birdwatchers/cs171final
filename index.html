Miles from Home

Every year, billions of birds execute one of nature's most extraordinary performances—journeys so demanding they represent biological marvels of navigation and endurance. Barn swallows weighing less than an ounce travel 7,250 miles annually between continents, covering up to 200 kilometers daily. Red knots fly up to 19,000 miles each year—roughly the distance from Earth toward the moon. These aren't random flights; they're essential connections linking ecosystems across hemispheres, ancient pathways carved into genetic memory over millions of years. Yet these routes face unprecedented threats from habitat loss, climate change, and human development. Explore the data below to witness these extraordinary journeys.
